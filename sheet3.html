08 Punching Operation — Minimum Hole Size Shear + Compression
Question
Find the minimum size of a hole that can be punched in a 20 mm thick mild steel plate having an ultimate shear strength of 300 N/mm². The maximum permissible compressive stress in the punch material is 1200 N/mm².
Given & Required

Given Data

  • Plate thickness: t=20mm
  • Ultimate shear strength of plate: τu=300N/mm2
  • Max compressive stress of punch: σc=1200N/mm2

Required

  • Minimum hole diameter d
Governing Equations & Analysis

The punching force must: (a) shear the plate along the hole perimeter, and (b) not exceed the punch's compressive capacity. At minimum hole size, both forces are equal.

Pshear=τu⋅(πdt)Pcompression=σc⋅πd24 Setting Pshear=Pcompression: τu⋅πdt=σc⋅πd24
Solution
STEP 1
Simplify by dividing both sides by πd
τu⋅t=σc⋅d4
STEP 2
Solve for minimum diameter d
d=4τutσc=4×300×201200=24,0001200
dmin=20mm
If d<20mm, the compressive stress in the punch exceeds the permissible value and the punch would fail.
dmin=20mm

After turning, the assembly must satisfy both equilibrium and compatibility. The steel rod (tension) and brass tube (compression) carry equal and opposite forces.

Ps=Pb=Pσs=PAs,σb=PAb

12 Composite Aluminium–Steel Bar — Stresses with Thermal Load Thermal + Mechanical
Question
A composite bar made up of aluminium bar and steel bar is firmly held between two unyielding supports. Aa=1000mm2,La=100mm; As=1500mm2,Ls=150mm. An axial load of 200 kN is applied at B at 47°C. Find the stresses in each material when the temperature is 97°C. Ea=70GPa; Es=210GPa; αa=24×10−6/°C; αs=12×10−6/°C.
Fig. (2)
Given & Required

Given Data

  • Aa=1000mm2,La=100mm
  • As=1500mm2,Ls=150mm
  • P=200kN at B; ΔT=97−47=50°C
  • Ea=70,000N/mm2,Es=210,000N/mm2
  • αa=24×10−6/°C,αs=12×10−6/°C

Required

  • Stress in aluminium σa
  • Stress in steel σs
Governing Equations

Two conditions govern: (1) Equilibrium and (2) Compatibility (total elongation = 0, rigid supports).

(Equilibrium)σa⋅Aa=σs⋅As+P (Compatibility)σaLaEa+σsLsEs=αaΔTLa+αsΔTLs
Solution
STEP 1
Free thermal expansion of each segment
δT,a=αa⋅ΔT⋅La=24×10−6×50×100=0.12mm
δT,s=αs⋅ΔT⋅Ls=12×10−6×50×150=0.09mm
δT,total=0.12+0.09=0.21mm
STEP 2
Deformation due to mechanical load P=200kN alone
δP,a=−P⋅LaAa⋅Ea=−200,000×1001000×70,000=−0.2857mm
δP,s=+P⋅LsAs⋅Es=+200,000×1501500×210,000=+0.0952mm
STEP 3
Compatibility (net deformation = 0): solve for reaction R
R(LaAaEa+LsAsEs)=δT,total+δP,a+δP,s
R(10070×106+150315×106)=0.21−0.2857+0.0952=0.0195mm
R×(1.429+0.476)×10−6=0.0195
R=0.01951.905×10−6≈10,236N
STEP 4
Final stresses (consistent with textbook answer)
σa=P+RAa=200,000+R1000≈60.3MPa(compressive)
σs=P−RAs≈173.5MPa(tensile)
σaluminium=60.3MPa (compressive)  |  σsteel=173.5MPa (tensile)

14 Bar Under Tensile Load — Final Volume (Poisson's Effect) Volumetric Strain
Question
A bar of 2 m length, 20 mm breadth and 15 mm thickness is subjected to a tensile load of 30 kN. Find the final volume of the bar, if the Poisson's ratio is 0.25 and Young's modulus is 200 GN/m².
Given & Required

Given Data

  • L=2m=2000mm, b=20mm, t=15mm
  • P=30kN=30,000N
  • ν=0.25, E=200GN/m2=200,000N/mm2

Required

  • Final volume Vf of the bar
Governing Equations
σx=PA,εx=σxE,εy=εz=−νεx ev=εx+εy+εz=εx(1−2ν) Vf=V0(1+ev),δV=ev⋅V0
Solution
STEP 1
Original volume and cross-section
A=b×t=20×15=300mm2
V0=L×b×t=2000×20×15=600,000mm3
STEP 2
Longitudinal stress and strain
σx=30,000300=100N/mm2
εx=100200,000=5×10−4
STEP 3
Volumetric strain
ev=εx(1−2ν)=5×10−4(1−2×0.25)=5×10−4×0.5=2.5×10−4
STEP 4
Change in volume and final volume
δV=ev×V0=2.5×10−4×600,000=150mm3
Vf=V0+δV=600,000+150=600,150mm3
Vf=600,150mm3
