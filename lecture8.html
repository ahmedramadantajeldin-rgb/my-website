1 Material Used for Shafts

اختيار مادة ال shaft من أهم خطوات ال design، لأن المادة هي المسؤولة عن تحمّل ال twisting و ال bending و ال axial loads أثناء التشغيل.

Required Properties of Shaft Materials

لازم مادة ال shaft تتوفّر فيها ال properties التالية حتى تقدر تشتغل بكفاءة تحت ال loads المختلفة:

  1. High strength — مقاومة عالية حتى يتحمل ال stresses الناتجة من ال torque و ال bending moments بدون failure.
  2. Good machinability — سهولة التشغيل والتصنيع على المخارط وآلات ال CNC لتشكيل ال keyways، ال grooves، وال steps.
  3. Low notch sensitivity factor — عامل sensitivity منخفض عند وجود notches أو keyways، لأن ال stress concentration بيقل تأثيره في المادة ذات ال low notch sensitivity.
  4. Good heat treatment properties — قابلية جيدة للمعالجة الحرارية مثل ال hardening، ال tempering، و ال case hardening لتحسين ال surface hardness و ال wear resistance.
  5. High wear resistance — مقاومة عالية للتآكل خاصة عند أماكن ال bearings، ال seals، و ال gears.

Common Steel Grades for Ordinary Shafts

الشائع في ال ordinary shafts استخدام carbon steel بدرجات: 40 C 8، 45 C 8، 50 C 4، و 50 C 12. أما في حالات ال high strength فنستخدم alloy steels مثل nickel، nickel–chromium، أو chrome–vanadium steel.

2 Design of Shafts — Basis

ال shafts ممكن تتصمم على أساس Strength أو على أساس Rigidity & Stiffness. وفي كل حالة عندنا أكثر من loading case.

Design Criteria

  1. Strength-based design — بنصمم ال shaft بحيث ال stresses الناتجة تكون أقل من ال allowable stress للمادة.
  2. Rigidity & Stiffness-based design — بنصمم ال shaft بحيث ال deflection (سواء torsional أو lateral) أقل من قيمة مسموح بها.

Loading Cases Considered in Strength Design

Case (a) Twisting moment (torque) only
ال shaft بس بيتعرض ل torque — مثل ال transmission shafts البسيطة.
Case (b) Bending moment only
ال shaft بيتعرض ل bending فقط — مثل axles السيارة غير الدوارة.
Case (c) Combined twisting & bending
ال shaft بيحمل gears أو pulleys، فبيبقى عليه ال torque + bending معاً.
Case (d) Axial + combined torsion & bending
في ال propeller shafts و worm drives نضيف ال axial load.

3 Shafts Subjected to Twisting Moment Only

لما ال shaft يكون تحت torque only، بنستخدم ال torsion equation الأساسية لإيجاد ال diameter.

Torsion Equation

المعادلة الأساسية لل torsion:

$$ \frac{T}{J} = \frac{\tau}{r} $$
T
Twisting moment (torque) acting on the shaft, in N·mm
J
Polar moment of inertia about the axis of rotation
τ
Torsional shear stress, in N/mm²
r
Distance from neutral axis to outermost fibre = d/2

For a Round Solid Shaft

ال polar moment of inertia:

$$ J = \frac{\pi}{32}\, d^{4} $$

بالتعويض في ال torsion equation نحصل على:

$$ \boxed{\; T = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,d^{3} \;} $$

ومنها يمكن إيجاد قطر ال solid shaft.

For a Hollow Shaft

ال polar moment لل hollow shaft:

$$ J = \frac{\pi}{32}\left[(d_o)^{4} - (d_i)^{4}\right] $$

حيث do و di هما ال outside و ال inside diameters، و r = do/2. بتعريف k = di/do:

$$ T = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\left[\frac{(d_o)^{4} - (d_i)^{4}}{d_o}\right] = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,(d_o)^{3}\,(1 - k^{4}) $$
Important Notes
  • Hollow shafts — بتستخدم في ال marine work. أقوى لكل kg من المادة، وبتتشكل على mandrel فتكون المادة أكثر homogeneous.
  • Equal strength (hollow = solid): $\;(d_o)^{3}(1 - k^{4}) = d^{3}\;$
  • Power transmitted (in Watts): $$ P = \frac{2\pi N\, T}{60} \quad\Rightarrow\quad T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} $$ حيث N سرعة ال shaft بال r.p.m.
  • In belt drives: $\; T = (T_{1} - T_{2})\,R \;$
    حيث T1 و T2 هم ال tight side و ال slack side tensions، و R نصف قطر ال pulley.
1

Line Shaft — Diameter for Torque Only

Problem A line shaft rotating at 200 r.p.m. is to transmit 20 kW. The shaft may be assumed to be made of mild steel with an allowable shear stress of 42 MPa. Determine the diameter of the shaft, neglecting the bending moment on the shaft.
Solution

Given: N = 200 r.p.m., P = 20 kW = 20 × 10³ W, τ = 42 N/mm²

Step 1 — حساب ال torque الناتج من ال power:

$$ T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{20 \times 10^{3} \times 60}{2\pi \times 200} = 955 \text{ N·m} = 955 \times 10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 2 — تطبيق معادلة ال torsion لإيجاد القطر:

$$ T = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,d^{3} $$ $$ 955 \times 10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 42 \times d^{3} = 8.25\, d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = \frac{955 \times 10^{3}}{8.25} = 115\,733 \;\Rightarrow\; d = 48.7 \text{ mm} $$

Answer: d ≈ 50 mm

2

Solid Shaft — Max Torque Exceeds Mean Torque

Problem A solid shaft is transmitting 1 MW at 240 r.p.m. Determine the diameter of the shaft if the maximum torque transmitted exceeds the mean torque by 20%. Take the maximum allowable shear stress as 60 MPa.
Solution

Given: P = 1 × 10⁶ W, N = 240 r.p.m., Tmax = 1.2 Tmean, τ = 60 N/mm²

Step 1 — حساب ال mean torque:

$$ T_{mean} = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{1 \times 10^{6} \times 60}{2\pi \times 240} = 39\,784 \text{ N·m} $$

Step 2 — حساب ال maximum torque:

$$ T_{max} = 1.2 \times T_{mean} = 1.2 \times 39\,784 \times 10^{3} = 47\,741 \times 10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 3 — تطبيق معادلة ال torsion باستخدام ال Tmax:

$$ T_{max} = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,d^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; 47\,741 \times 10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 60 \times d^{3} = 11.78\, d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 4053 \times 10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 159.4 \text{ mm} $$

Answer: d ≈ 160 mm

3

Solid vs Hollow Shaft — Same Power

Problem Find the diameter of a solid steel shaft to transmit 20 kW at 200 r.p.m. The ultimate shear stress for the steel may be taken as 360 MPa and a factor of safety as 8. If a hollow shaft is to be used in place of the solid shaft, find the inside and outside diameter when the ratio of inside to outside diameters is 0.5.
Solution

Given: P = 20 kW, N = 200 r.p.m., τu = 360 N/mm², F.S. = 8, k = di/do = 0.5

Step 1 — حساب ال allowable shear stress:

$$ \tau = \frac{\tau_{u}}{F.S.} = \frac{360}{8} = 45 \text{ N/mm}^{2} $$

▸ Solid Shaft Diameter

Step 2 — ال torque:

$$ T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{20 \times 10^{3} \times 60}{2\pi \times 200} = 955 \text{ N·m} = 955 \times 10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$
$$ 955 \times 10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 45 \times d^{3} = 8.84\, d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 108\,032 \;\Rightarrow\; d = 47.6 \text{ mm} \approx 50 \text{ mm} $$

▸ Hollow Shaft Diameters

Step 3 — تطبيق معادلة ال hollow shaft:

$$ T = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,(d_o)^{3}\,(1 - k^{4}) $$ $$ 955 \times 10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 45 \times (d_o)^{3} \times \left[1 - (0.5)^{4}\right] = 8.3\,(d_o)^{3} $$ $$ (d_o)^{3} = 115\,060 \;\Rightarrow\; d_o = 48.6 \text{ mm} \approx 50 \text{ mm} $$ $$ d_i = 0.5 \times d_o = 0.5 \times 50 = 25 \text{ mm} $$

Answers: do = 50 mm    di = 25 mm

4 Shafts Subjected to Bending Moment Only

في هذه الحالة بيكون ال shaft تحت bending moment فقط (زي ال non-rotating axles)، فنستخدم ال bending equation.

Bending Equation
$$ \frac{M}{I} = \frac{\sigma_{b}}{y} $$
M
Bending moment in N·mm
I
Moment of inertia of cross-sectional area about axis of rotation
σb
Bending stress (tensile or compressive)
y
Distance from neutral axis to outer-most fibre

For a Round Solid Shaft

ال moment of inertia لل circular cross-section:

$$ I = \frac{\pi}{64}\,d^{4}, \qquad y = \frac{d}{2} $$

وبالتعويض:

$$ \boxed{\; M = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,d^{3} \;} $$

For a Hollow Shaft

$$ I = \frac{\pi}{64}\left[(d_o)^{4} - (d_i)^{4}\right] = \frac{\pi}{64}\,(d_o)^{4}(1 - k^{4}) $$ $$ y = \frac{d_o}{2} $$
$$ M = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,(d_o)^{3}(1 - k^{4}) $$

ومنها يُحسب ال outside diameter.

5 Shafts Subjected to Combined Twisting & Bending

أغلب ال shafts الحقيقية (مثل اللي تحمل gears أو pulleys) بيكون عليها twisting و bending في نفس الوقت، فلازم نصممها على أساس الاثنين معاً باستخدام إحدى failure theories.

Two Major Failure Theories

  1. Maximum Shear Stress Theory (Guest's Theory) — تستخدم لل ductile materials مثل mild steel.
  2. Maximum Normal Stress Theory (Rankine's Theory) — تستخدم لل brittle materials مثل cast iron.

Let: τ = shear stress from twisting, and σb = bending stress from bending moment.

1) Maximum Shear Stress Theory (Guest's)

بحسب هذه النظرية، أقصى shear stress في ال shaft:

$$ \tau_{max} = \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{(\sigma_{b})^{2} + 4\tau^{2}} $$

بتعويض σb = 32M/(πd³) و τ = 16T/(πd³):

$$ \tau_{max} = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\left(\frac{32M}{\pi d^{3}}\right)^{2} + 4\left(\frac{16T}{\pi d^{3}}\right)^{2}} = \frac{16}{\pi d^{3}}\sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} $$

أي:

$$ \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau_{max}\,d^{3} = \sqrt{M^{2} + T^{2}} $$
Equivalent Twisting Moment

Te = ال twisting moment اللي لو اشتغل لوحده سينتج نفس τ الفعلي:

$$ \boxed{\; T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2} + T^{2}} = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,d^{3} \;} $$

ومنها يُحسب ال diameter.

2) Maximum Normal Stress Theory (Rankine's)

أقصى normal stress:

$$ \sigma_{b(max)} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\sigma_{b} + \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{(\sigma_{b})^{2} + 4\tau^{2}} $$

بالتعويض:

$$ \sigma_{b(max)} = \frac{32}{\pi d^{3}}\left[\tfrac{1}{2}(M + \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}})\right] $$
Equivalent Bending Moment

Me = ال moment اللي لو اشتغل لوحده سينتج نفس σb:

$$ \boxed{\; M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[M + \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}}\right] = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,d^{3} \;} $$

For Hollow Shafts

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,(d_{o})^{3}(1-k^{4}) $$ $$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[M + \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}}\right] = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,(d_{o})^{3}(1-k^{4}) $$
Rule يُفضّل حساب قطر ال shaft باستخدام النظريتين معاً واختيار أكبر قيمة للأمان.
1

Solid Circular Shaft — 45 C 8 Steel

Problem A solid circular shaft is subjected to a bending moment of 3000 N·m and a torque of 10 000 N·m. The shaft is made of 45 C 8 steel having ultimate tensile stress of 700 MPa and an ultimate shear stress of 500 MPa. Assuming a factor of safety as 6, determine the diameter of the shaft.
Solution

Given: M = 3 × 10⁶ N·mm, T = 10 × 10⁶ N·mm, σtu = 700 N/mm², τu = 500 N/mm², F.S. = 6

Step 1 — حساب ال allowable stresses:

$$ \sigma_{t} = \sigma_{b} = \frac{\sigma_{tu}}{F.S.} = \frac{700}{6} = 116.7 \text{ N/mm}^{2} $$ $$ \tau = \frac{\tau_{u}}{F.S.} = \frac{500}{6} = 83.3 \text{ N/mm}^{2} $$

▸ Using Maximum Shear Stress Theory

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} = \sqrt{(3\times10^{6})^{2} + (10\times10^{6})^{2}} = 10.44 \times 10^{6} \text{ N·mm} $$ $$ T_{e} = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,d^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; 10.44\times10^{6} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 83.3 \times d^{3} = 16.36\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 0.636 \times 10^{6} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 86 \text{ mm} $$

▸ Using Maximum Normal Stress Theory

$$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}(M + T_{e}) = \tfrac{1}{2}(3\times10^{6} + 10.44\times10^{6}) = 6.72\times10^{6} \text{ N·mm} $$ $$ M_{e} = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,d^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; 6.72\times10^{6} = \frac{\pi}{32} \times 116.7 \times d^{3} = 11.46\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 0.586\times10^{6} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 83.7 \text{ mm} $$

Taking the larger of the two values:

Answer: d ≈ 90 mm

2

Shaft with Central Gear Between Bearings

Problem A shaft supported at the ends in ball bearings carries a straight tooth spur gear at its mid span and is to transmit 7.5 kW at 300 r.p.m. The pitch circle diameter of the gear is 150 mm. The distances between the center line of bearings and gear are 100 mm each. If the shaft is made of steel and the allowable shear stress is 45 MPa, determine the diameter of the shaft. Show how the gear will be mounted and indicate bearing ends. The pressure angle of the gear may be taken as 20°.
Solution

Given: P = 7500 W, N = 300 r.p.m., D = 150 mm, L = 200 mm, τ = 45 N/mm², α = 20°

Step 1 — ال torque المنقول من ال shaft:

$$ T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{7500 \times 60}{2\pi \times 300} = 238.7 \text{ N·m} $$

Step 2 — ال tangential force على ال gear:

$$ F_{t} = \frac{2T}{D} = \frac{2 \times 238.7}{0.15} = 3182.7 \text{ N} $$

Step 3 — ال normal load على ال tooth (مع مراعاة ال pressure angle):

$$ W = \frac{F_{t}}{\cos\alpha} = \frac{3182.7}{\cos 20°} = \frac{3182.7}{0.9397} = 3387 \text{ N} $$

Step 4 — لأن ال gear في منتصف ال shaft، أقصى bending moment في المنتصف:

$$ M = \frac{W \cdot L}{4} = \frac{3387 \times 0.2}{4} = 169.4 \text{ N·m} $$

Step 5 — ال equivalent twisting moment:

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} = \sqrt{(169.4)^{2} + (238.7)^{2}} = 292.7 \text{ N·m} = 292.7\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 6 — إيجاد ال diameter:

$$ 292.7\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 45 \times d^{3} = 8.84\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 33 \times 10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 32 \text{ mm} $$

Answer: d ≈ 35 mm

3

Shaft with Two Pulleys — Vertical + Horizontal Loads

Problem A shaft is supported by two bearings placed 1 m apart. A 600 mm diameter pulley is mounted at a distance of 300 mm to the right of the left hand bearing and this drives a pulley directly below it with the help of a belt having maximum tension of 2.25 kN. Another pulley 400 mm diameter is placed 200 mm to the left of the right hand bearing and is driven with the help of an electric motor and belt, placed horizontally to the right. The angle of contact for both pulleys is 180° and μ = 0.24. Determine the suitable diameter for a solid shaft, allowing working stress of 63 MPa in tension and 42 MPa in shear. Assume torque on one pulley equals torque on the other.
Solution

Given: AB = 1 m, DC = 600 mm, AC = 300 mm, T1 = 2250 N, DD = 400 mm, BD = 200 mm, θ = π rad, μ = 0.24, σb = 63 N/mm², τ = 42 N/mm²

▸ Step 1: Belt Tensions on Pulley C (Vertical Belt)

$$ 2.3 \log\!\left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right) = \mu\theta = 0.24 \times \pi = 0.754 $$ $$ \log\!\left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right) = \frac{0.754}{2.3} = 0.3278 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}} = 2.127 $$ $$ T_{2} = \frac{2250}{2.127} = 1058 \text{ N} $$

Vertical load at C:

$$ W_{C} = T_{1} + T_{2} = 2250 + 1058 = 3308 \text{ N} $$

Vertical load at D = 0 (because belt on D is horizontal).

▸ Step 2: Torque Acting on the Shaft

$$ T = (T_{1} - T_{2})\,R_{C} = (2250 - 1058) \times 0.3 = 357.6 \text{ N·m} $$

▸ Step 3: Belt Tensions on Pulley D (Horizontal Belt)

بما أن ال torque نفسه على ال pulley D:

$$ (T_{3} - T_{4})\,R_{D} = 357.6 \;\Rightarrow\; T_{3} - T_{4} = \frac{357.6}{0.2} = 1788 \text{ N} $$ $$ \frac{T_{3}}{T_{4}} = 2.127 \;\Rightarrow\; T_{3} = 2.127\,T_{4} $$

بحل المعادلتين:

$$ T_{3} = 3376 \text{ N}, \qquad T_{4} = 1588 \text{ N} $$

Horizontal load at D:

$$ W_{D} = T_{3} + T_{4} = 3376 + 1588 = 4964 \text{ N} $$

Horizontal load at C = 0.

▸ Step 4: Reactions & B.M. — Vertical Loading

بأخذ ال moments about A:

$$ R_{BV} \times 1 = 3308 \times 0.3 \;\Rightarrow\; R_{BV} = 992.4 \text{ N} $$ $$ R_{AV} = 3308 - 992.4 = 2315.6 \text{ N} $$

Vertical B.M. at C & D:

$$ M_{CV} = R_{AV} \times 0.3 = 2315.6 \times 0.3 = 694.7 \text{ N·m} $$ $$ M_{DV} = R_{BV} \times 0.2 = 992.4 \times 0.2 = 198.5 \text{ N·m} $$

▸ Step 5: Reactions & B.M. — Horizontal Loading

$$ R_{BH} \times 1 = 4964 \times 0.8 \;\Rightarrow\; R_{BH} = 3971 \text{ N} $$ $$ R_{AH} = 4964 - 3971 = 993 \text{ N} $$

Horizontal B.M. at C & D:

$$ M_{CH} = R_{AH} \times 0.3 = 993 \times 0.3 = 297.9 \text{ N·m} $$ $$ M_{DH} = R_{BH} \times 0.2 = 3971 \times 0.2 = 794.2 \text{ N·m} $$

▸ Step 6: Resultant Bending Moments

$$ M_{C} = \sqrt{(M_{CV})^{2} + (M_{CH})^{2}} = \sqrt{(694.7)^{2} + (297.9)^{2}} = 756 \text{ N·m} $$ $$ M_{D} = \sqrt{(M_{DV})^{2} + (M_{DH})^{2}} = \sqrt{(198.5)^{2} + (794.2)^{2}} = 819.2 \text{ N·m} $$

أقصى bending moment عند D: M = 819.2 N·m

▸ Step 7: Diameter — Using Both Theories

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} = \sqrt{(819.2)^{2} + (357.6)^{2}} = 894 \text{ N·m} $$

Shear stress theory:

$$ 894\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 42 \times d^{3} = 8.25\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 108 \times 10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 47.6 \text{ mm} $$
$$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}(M + T_{e}) = \tfrac{1}{2}(819.2 + 894) = 856.6 \text{ N·m} $$

Normal stress theory:

$$ 856.6\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{32} \times 63 \times d^{3} = 6.2\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 138.2 \times 10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 51.7 \text{ mm} $$

Taking the larger value:

Answer: d ≈ 55 mm

6 Shafts Subjected to Fluctuating Loads

في ال practice، ال torque و ال bending moment مش ثابتين، بل fluctuating. لذا لازم نضيف shock & fatigue factors على القيم المحسوبة.

Modified Equivalent Moments

ال equivalent twisting moment:

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{(K_{m}\cdot M)^{2} + (K_{t}\cdot T)^{2}} $$

ال equivalent bending moment:

$$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[K_{m}\cdot M + \sqrt{(K_{m}\cdot M)^{2} + (K_{t}\cdot T)^{2}}\right] $$
Km
Combined shock and fatigue factor for bending
Kt
Combined shock and fatigue factor for torsion
Table 14.2 — Recommended Values for Km and Kt
Nature of LoadKmKt
1. Stationary Shafts
(a) Gradually applied load1.01.0
(b) Suddenly applied load1.5 – 2.01.5 – 2.0
2. Rotating Shafts
(a) Gradually applied or steady load1.51.0
(b) Suddenly applied load with minor shocks only1.5 – 2.01.5 – 2.0
(c) Suddenly applied load with heavy shocks2.0 – 3.01.5 – 3.0
1

Mild Steel Shaft — With & Without Shock Factors

Problem A mild steel shaft transmits 20 kW at 200 r.p.m. It carries a central load of 900 N and is simply supported between bearings 2.5 metres apart. Determine the size of the shaft, if the allowable shear stress is 42 MPa and the maximum tensile or compressive stress is not to exceed 56 MPa. What size of shaft will be required if it is subjected to gradually applied loads?
Solution

Given: P = 20 kW, N = 200 r.p.m., W = 900 N, L = 2.5 m, τ = 42 N/mm², σb = 56 N/mm²

▸ Part A: Without Shock Factors (Km = Kt = 1)

Step 1 — ال torque:

$$ T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{20\times10^{3} \times 60}{2\pi \times 200} = 955 \text{ N·m} = 955\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 2 — أقصى bending moment لحمل مركزي على simply supported shaft:

$$ M = \frac{W \times L}{4} = \frac{900 \times 2.5}{4} = 562.5 \text{ N·m} = 562.5\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 3 — ال equivalent twisting moment:

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{M^{2}+T^{2}} = \sqrt{(562.5\times10^{3})^{2} + (955\times10^{3})^{2}} = 1108\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$
$$ 1108\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 42 \times d^{3} = 8.25\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 134.3\times10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 51.2 \text{ mm} $$

Step 4 — ال equivalent bending moment:

$$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}(M + T_{e}) = \tfrac{1}{2}(562.5\times10^{3} + 1108\times10^{3}) = 835.25\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$
$$ 835.25\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{32} \times 56 \times d^{3} = 5.5\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 152\times10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 53.4 \text{ mm} $$

Taking the larger: d ≈ 55 mm

▸ Part B: With Gradually Applied Loads — Km = 1.5, Kt = 1.0

من Table 14.2 لل rotating shafts مع gradually applied load.

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{(K_{m}M)^{2} + (K_{t}T)^{2}} $$ $$ T_{e} = \sqrt{(1.5 \times 562.5\times10^{3})^{2} + (1 \times 955\times10^{3})^{2}} = 1274\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$
$$ 1274\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 42 \times d^{3} = 8.25\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 154.6\times10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 53.6 \text{ mm} $$
$$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[K_{m}M + T_{e}\right] = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[1.5\times562.5\times10^{3} + 1274\times10^{3}\right] = 1059\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$
$$ 1059\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{32} \times 56 \times d^{3} = 5.5\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 192.5\times10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 57.7 \text{ mm} $$

Taking the larger: d ≈ 60 mm

2

Shaft with Overhung Pulley — Motor → Lathe

Problem Design a shaft to transmit power from an electric motor to a lathe head stock through a pulley by means of a belt drive. The pulley weighs 200 N and is located at 300 mm from the centre of the bearing. The diameter of the pulley is 200 mm and the maximum power transmitted is 1 kW at 120 r.p.m. The angle of lap of the belt is 180° and coefficient of friction between the belt and pulley is 0.3. The shock and fatigue factors for bending and twisting are 1.5 and 2.0 respectively. The allowable shear stress in the shaft may be taken as 35 MPa.
Solution

Given: W = 200 N, L = 300 mm, D = 200 mm, R = 100 mm, P = 1000 W, N = 120 r.p.m., θ = π rad, μ = 0.3, Km = 1.5, Kt = 2, τ = 35 N/mm²

Step 1 — ال torque:

$$ T = \frac{P \times 60}{2\pi N} = \frac{1000 \times 60}{2\pi \times 120} = 79.6 \text{ N·m} = 79.6\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 2 — فرق ال belt tensions:

$$ T = (T_{1} - T_{2})\,R \;\Rightarrow\; T_{1} - T_{2} = \frac{79.6\times10^{3}}{100} = 796 \text{ N} $$

Step 3 — نسبة ال tensions:

$$ 2.3\log\!\left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right) = \mu\theta = 0.3\pi = 0.9426 $$ $$ \log\!\left(\frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}}\right) = 0.4098 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{T_{1}}{T_{2}} = 2.57 $$

Step 4 — حل المعادلتين:

$$ T_{1} = 1303 \text{ N}, \qquad T_{2} = 507 \text{ N} $$

Step 5 — ال total vertical load على ال pulley:

$$ W_{T} = T_{1} + T_{2} + W = 1303 + 507 + 200 = 2010 \text{ N} $$

Step 6 — ال bending moment (للعمود ال cantilever):

$$ M = W_{T} \times L = 2010 \times 300 = 603\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 7 — ال equivalent twisting moment مع ال shock factors:

$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{(K_{m}M)^{2} + (K_{t}T)^{2}} $$ $$ T_{e} = \sqrt{(1.5 \times 603\times10^{3})^{2} + (2 \times 79.6\times10^{3})^{2}} = 918\times10^{3} \text{ N·mm} $$

Step 8 — إيجاد ال diameter:

$$ 918\times10^{3} = \frac{\pi}{16} \times 35 \times d^{3} = 6.87\,d^{3} $$ $$ d^{3} = 133.6\times10^{3} \;\Rightarrow\; d = 51.1 \text{ mm} $$

Answer: d ≈ 55 mm

7 Axial Load + Combined Torsion & Bending

في حالة وجود axial load F بالإضافة لل torsion و ال bending (مثل propeller shafts في السفن و ال worm gear shafts)، لازم نضيف ال axial stress لل bending stress.

Resultant Stress Formulation

ال bending stress:

$$ \sigma_{b} = \frac{M \cdot y}{I} = \frac{32M}{\pi d^{3}} $$

ال stress due to axial load:

$$ \sigma_{a} = \frac{F}{\tfrac{\pi}{4}d^{2}} = \frac{4F}{\pi d^{2}} \qquad \text{(solid)} $$ $$ \sigma_{a} = \frac{4F}{\pi\left[(d_o)^{2} - (d_i)^{2}\right]} = \frac{F}{\pi (d_o)^{2}(1 - k^{2})} \qquad \text{(hollow)} $$

Resultant stress (solid shaft):

$$ \sigma_{1} = \frac{32M}{\pi d^{3}} + \frac{4F}{\pi d^{2}} = \frac{32}{\pi d^{3}}\left(M + \frac{F \cdot d}{8}\right) = \frac{32\, M_{1}}{\pi d^{3}} $$

حيث: $\;M_{1} = M + \dfrac{F \cdot d}{8}\;$ (Modified bending moment)

Resultant stress (hollow shaft):

$$ \sigma_{1} = \frac{32\, M_{1}}{\pi (d_{o})^{3}(1 - k^{4})}, \qquad M_{1} = M + \frac{F \, d_{o}\,(1 + k^{2})}{8} $$
Column Factor α (for Slender Shafts in Compression)

عند ال long slender shafts المعرضة ل compressive loads، لازم نضيف column factor α لأخذ buckling effect في الاعتبار.

Stress due to compressive load:

$$ \sigma_{c} = \frac{\alpha \times 4F}{\pi d^{2}} \qquad \text{(solid)}; \quad \sigma_{c} = \frac{\alpha \times 4F}{\pi (d_o)^{2}(1 - k^{2})} \quad \text{(hollow)} $$

Column Factor Formulae

عند slenderness ratio L/K < 115:

$$ \alpha = \frac{1}{1 - 0.0044\,(L/K)} $$

عند L/K > 115:

$$ \alpha = \frac{\sigma_{y}\,(L/K)^{2}}{C\,\pi^{2}\,E} $$
L
Length of shaft between the bearings
K
Least radius of gyration
σy
Compressive yield point stress of shaft material
C
Euler coefficient depending on end conditions

Values of C for Different End Conditions

End ConditionC
Hinged ends1
Fixed ends2.25
Partly restrained (as in bearings)1.6
General Formula لل hollow shaft ال معرض ل fluctuating torsion، bending، و axial load معاً:
$$ T_{e} = \sqrt{\left[K_{m}M + \frac{\alpha F\, d_{o}(1 + k^{2})}{8}\right]^{2} + (K_{t}T)^{2}} = \frac{\pi}{16}\,\tau\,(d_o)^{3}(1 - k^{4}) $$ $$ M_{e} = \tfrac{1}{2}\left[K_{m}M + \frac{\alpha F\, d_{o}(1+k^{2})}{8} + \sqrt{\left\{K_{m}M + \frac{\alpha F d_o (1+k^2)}{8}\right\}^{2} + (K_{t}T)^{2}}\right] = \frac{\pi}{32}\,\sigma_{b}\,(d_o)^{3}(1 - k^{4}) $$

Special cases: لل solid shaft k = 0 و do = d. بدون axial load F = 0. ولو ال axial load tensile فقط فإن α = 1.

8 Design of Shafts on Basis of Rigidity

في بعض الحالات لا يكفي التصميم على أساس ال strength، بل لازم نضمن أن ال deflection (torsional أو lateral) لا تتجاوز قيمة مسموح بها.

1) Torsional Rigidity

ال torsional rigidity مهمة في camshafts محركات ال I.C. engines لأن ال twist بيأثر على valve timing.

Permissible Twist Limits
  • Camshafts (I.C. engines): لا يتجاوز 0.25° / metre.
  • Line shafts / Transmission shafts: من 2.5° إلى 3° / metre.
  • Widely-used deflection limit: 1° في طول = 20 × d (أي 20 ضعف القطر).

Torsional Deflection Equation

$$ \frac{T}{J} = \frac{G \cdot \theta}{L} \;\Rightarrow\; \boxed{\; \theta = \frac{T \cdot L}{J \cdot G} \;} $$
θ
Torsional deflection / angle of twist (radians)
T
Twisting moment / torque on the shaft
J
Polar moment of inertia of cross-sectional area:
• Solid: $J = \tfrac{\pi}{32}\,d^{4}$
• Hollow: $J = \tfrac{\pi}{32}\left[(d_{o})^{4} - (d_{i})^{4}\right]$
G
Modulus of rigidity of shaft material
L
Length of the shaft

2) Lateral Rigidity

ال lateral rigidity مهمة في:

  • Transmission shafts و ال high-speed shafts — أي lateral deflection صغيرة تنتج out-of-balance forces كبيرة جداً.
  • المحافظة على bearing clearances الصحيحة.
  • محاذاة gear teeth بشكل صحيح (gear teeth alignment).

Deflection Calculation Approach

Uniform Shaft cross-section ثابت → استخدام deflection formulae من Strength of Materials مباشرة.
Variable Shaft cross-section متغير → استخدام المعادلة التفاضلية الأساسية ل elastic curve.

المعادلة التفاضلية الأساسية ل beam deflection:

$$ \frac{d^{2}y}{dx^{2}} = \frac{M}{E \cdot I} $$
y
Lateral deflection at position x
M
Bending moment at section x
E
Modulus of elasticity of shaft material
I
Second moment of area (moment of inertia)

تُحل هذه المعادلة بالتكامل المزدوج مع تطبيق ال boundary conditions للحصول على شكل ال elastic curve و أقصى deflection.
