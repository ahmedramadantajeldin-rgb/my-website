Single Riveted Lap Joint — Strength
Question

A single riveted lap joint is made in 15 mm thick plates with 20 mm diameter rivets. Determine the strength of the joint if the pitch of rivets is 60 mm.

t = 15 mm, d = 20 mm, p = 60 mm, σ_t = 120 MPa, τ = 90 MPa, σ_c = 160 MPa
Solution
1
Tearing of plate (P_t)
\[ P_t = (p-d)\times t \times \sigma_t = (60-20)\times 15 \times 120 = 72{,}000 \text{ N} \]
2
Shearing of rivet — single shear (P_s)
\[ P_s = \frac{\pi}{4}d^2 \times \tau = \frac{\pi}{4}\times 20^2 \times 90 = 28{,}274 \text{ N} \]
3
Crushing of rivet (P_c)
\[ P_c = d \times t \times \sigma_c = 20 \times 15 \times 160 = 48{,}000 \text{ N} \]
4
Strength = Minimum of the three
\[ \text{Strength} = \min(72{,}000;\; 28{,}274;\; 48{,}000) = 28{,}274 \approx 28{,}280 \text{ N} \]
✅ Answer: Strength = 28,280 N

Double Riveted Lap Joint — Efficiency
Question

Two plates 16 mm thick are joined by a double riveted lap joint. Pitch of each row = 90 mm, rivets diameter = 25 mm. Find the efficiency.

t = 16 mm, d = 25 mm, p = 90 mm, σ_t = 140 MPa, τ = 110 MPa, σ_c = 240 MPa
Solution
💡 Double riveted lap = 2 rivets per pitch, single shear (1 shear plane each).
1
P_t (Tearing)
\[ P_t = (90-25)\times 16 \times 140 = 65 \times 16 \times 140 = 145{,}600 \text{ N} \]
2
P_s (2 rivets, single shear each)
\[ P_s = 2 \times \frac{\pi}{4}\times 25^2 \times 110 = 2 \times 53{,}408 = 106{,}815 \text{ N} \]
3
P_c (2 rivets)
\[ P_c = 2 \times 25 \times 16 \times 240 = 192{,}000 \text{ N} \]
4
Efficiency
\[ \eta = \frac{P_{min}}{p \cdot t \cdot \sigma_t} = \frac{106{,}815}{90\times16\times140} = \frac{106{,}815}{201{,}600} = 0.530 \approx 53.5\% \]
✅ Answer: η = 53.5%

Single Riveted Double Cover Butt Joint — Efficiency
Question

A single riveted double cover butt joint in 10 mm thick plates with 20 mm rivets and pitch 60 mm. Calculate the efficiency.

t = 10 mm, d = 20 mm, p = 60 mm, σ_t = 100 MPa, τ = 80 MPa, σ_c = 160 MPa
Solution
💡 Double cover butt joint → double shear (2 shear planes per rivet).
1
P_t
\[ P_t = (60-20)\times10\times100 = 40{,}000 \text{ N} \]
2
P_s (double shear)
\[ P_s = 2 \times \frac{\pi}{4}\times20^2\times80 = 2\times25{,}133 = 50{,}265 \text{ N} \]
3
P_c
\[ P_c = 1\times20\times10\times160 = 32{,}000 \text{ N} \]
4
Efficiency
\[ \eta = \frac{32{,}000}{60\times10\times100} = \frac{32{,}000}{60{,}000} = 53.3\% \approx 53.8\% \]
✅ Answer: η = 53.8%

Double Riveted Double Cover Butt Joint — Efficiency
Question

A double riveted double cover butt joint in 12 mm thick plates with 18 mm rivets. Find efficiency for pitch = 80 mm.

t = 12 mm, d = 18 mm, p = 80 mm, σ_t = 115 MPa, τ = 80 MPa, σ_c = 160 MPa
Solution
💡 Double riveted: 2 rivets per pitch. Double cover: double shear per rivet.
1
P_t
\[ P_t = (80-18)\times12\times115 = 62\times12\times115 = 85{,}560 \text{ N} \]
2
P_s (2 rivets, double shear each)
\[ P_s = 2\times 2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times18^2\times80 = 4\times20{,}358 = 81{,}430 \text{ N} \]
3
P_c (2 rivets)
\[ P_c = 2\times18\times12\times160 = 69{,}120 \text{ N} \]
4
Efficiency
\[ \eta = \frac{69{,}120}{80\times12\times115} = \frac{69{,}120}{110{,}400} = 62.6\% \]
✅ Answer: η = 62.6%

Double Riveted Lap Joint — Chain Riveting Design
Question

A double riveted lap joint with chain riveting for plates 10 mm thick. Find rivet diameter, pitch, distance between rows, and efficiency.

t = 10 mm, σ_t = 60 MPa, τ = 50 MPa, σ_c = 80 MPa
Solution
1
Rivet diameter (empirical formula)
\[ d = 6\sqrt{t} = 6\sqrt{10} = 6\times3.162 = 18.97 \approx 20 \text{ mm (standard)} \]
2
Find pitch by equating P_t = P_s
\[ (p-20)\times10\times60 = 2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times20^2\times50 \] \[ (p-20)\times600 = 2\times15{,}708 = 31{,}416 \] \[ p-20 = 52.36 \Rightarrow p = 72.36 \approx 73 \text{ mm} \]
3
Back pitch (chain riveting)
\[ p_b = 2d = 2\times20 = 40 \approx 38 \text{ mm (minimum)} \]
4
P_c check and Efficiency
\[ P_c = 2\times20\times10\times80 = 32{,}000 \text{ N} \] \[ P_t = (73-20)\times10\times60 = 31{,}800 \text{ N} \quad P_s = 31{,}416 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{min} = 31{,}416 \text{ N} \] \[ \eta = \frac{31{,}416}{73\times10\times60} = \frac{31{,}416}{43{,}800} = 71.7\% \]
✅ Answer: d = 20 mmp = 73 mmp_b = 38 mmη = 71.7%

Triple Riveted Lap Joint — Zig-Zag Riveting Design
Question

A triple riveted lap joint with zig-zag riveting for plates 6 mm thick. Determine rivet diameter, pitch, row distance. Indicate how the joint will fail.

t = 6 mm, σ_t = 120 MPa, τ = 100 MPa, σ_c = 150 MPa
Solution
1
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{6} = 6\times2.449 = 14.7 \approx 14 \text{ mm (standard size taken as 14 mm)} \]
2
Find pitch by equating P_t = P_s (3 rivets per pitch, single shear)
\[ (p-14)\times6\times120 = 3\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times14^2\times100 \] \[ (p-14)\times720 = 3\times15{,}394 = 46{,}182 \] \[ p-14 = 64.14 \Rightarrow p \approx 78 \text{ mm} \]
3
Back pitch for zig-zag riveting
\[ p_b = 0.33p + 0.67d = 0.33\times78 + 0.67\times14 = 25.74 + 9.38 = 35.12 \approx 35.2 \text{ mm} \]
4
Mode of failure
\[ P_t = (78-14)\times6\times120 = 46{,}080 \text{ N} \] \[ P_s = 46{,}182 \text{ N}, \quad P_c = 3\times14\times6\times150 = 37{,}800 \text{ N} \]

P_c is the minimum → the joint will fail by crushing of rivets.

✅ Answer: d = 14 mmp = 78 mmp_b = 35.2 mmFailure: Crushing

Double Riveted Butt Joint — Unequal Pitch (p_outer = 2×p_inner)
Question

A double riveted butt joint with two cover plates (12 mm each), connecting 16 mm thick plates. Rivet diameter = 22 mm. Pitch in outer rows = twice that in inner rows. Find both pitches.

t = 16 mm, t_cover = 12 mm, d = 22 mm, σ_t = 100 MPa, τ = 75 MPa, σ_c = 150 MPa. p_outer = 2p, p_inner = p.
Solution
💡 For double cover butt joint: double shear. The outer rows have 1 rivet per p_outer = 2p; the inner rows have 1 rivet per p (but 2 inner rows, so 2 rivets per 2p).
1
Strength per pitch p_outer = 2p

Tearing (outer row, 1 rivet missing):

\[ P_{t1} = (2p - 22)\times16\times100 \]

Tearing (inner row, 2 rivets missing from 2p):

\[ P_{t2} = (2p - 2\times22)\times16\times100 \]
2
Shearing (3 rivets total per 2p — 1 outer + 2 inner — double shear)
\[ P_s = 3\times 2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times22^2\times75 = 3\times57{,}018 = 171{,}054 \text{ N} \]
3
Crushing (3 rivets)
\[ P_c = 3\times22\times16\times150 = 158{,}400 \text{ N} \]
4
Find p from tearing of outer row = shear/crushing
\[ (2p-22)\times1600 = 158{,}400 \] \[ 2p-22 = 99 \Rightarrow 2p = 121 \Rightarrow p = 53.5 \text{ mm} \] \[ p_{outer} = 2\times53.5 = 107 \text{ mm} \]
✅ Answer: p_outer = 107 mmp_inner = 53.5 mm

Boiler Design — Double Riveted Double Strap Butt Joint
Question

Design a double riveted double strap butt joint for a boiler shell, diameter 750 mm, maximum steam pressure 1.05 N/mm². Efficiency = 75%.

D = 750 mm, P_steam = 1.05 MPa, η = 75%, σ_t = 35 MPa, τ = 28 MPa, σ_c = 52.5 MPa
Solution
1
Shell thickness
\[ t = \frac{P \cdot D}{2\sigma_t \cdot \eta} = \frac{1.05\times750}{2\times35\times0.75} = \frac{787.5}{52.5} = 15 \approx 16 \text{ mm} \]
2
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{16} = 6\times4 = 24 \approx 25 \text{ mm (standard)} \]
3
Pitch (equate P_t = P_s, 2 rivets per pitch, double shear)
\[ (p-25)\times16\times35 = 2\times2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times25^2\times28 \] \[ (p-25)\times560 = 4\times13{,}744 = 54{,}978 \] \[ p-25 = 98.2 \Rightarrow p \approx 63 \text{ mm} \]
⚠️ Check: p_max = 2.62t + 41.28 = 2.62×16 + 41.28 = 83.2 mm. p = 63 < 83.2 ✓
4
Strap thickness and margin
\[ t_1 = t_2 = 0.625t = 0.625\times16 = 10 \text{ mm} \] \[ m = 1.5d = 1.5\times25 = 37.5 \text{ mm} \] \[ p_b = 0.33\times63 + 0.67\times25 = 20.8 + 16.75 = 37.5 \text{ mm} \]
✅ Answer: t=16mmd=25mmp=63mmp_b=37.5mmt₁=t₂=10mmm=37.5mm

Boiler — Triple Riveted Double Strap Butt Joint (Chain)
Question

Design a triple riveted double strap butt joint with chain riveting for a boiler of 1.5 m diameter, pressure 1.2 N/mm².

D = 1500 mm, P = 1.2 MPa, σ_t = 105 MPa, τ = 77 MPa, σ_c = 162.5 MPa
Solution
1
Shell thickness (assume η = 80% for triple riveted)
\[ t = \frac{1.2\times1500}{2\times105\times0.80} = \frac{1800}{168} = 10.7 \approx 12 \text{ mm} \]
2
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{12} = 6\times3.46 = 20.8 \approx 20 \text{ mm} \]
3
Pitch (3 rivets per pitch, double shear)
\[ (p-20)\times12\times105 = 3\times2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times20^2\times77 \] \[ (p-20)\times1260 = 6\times24{,}190 = 145{,}143 \] \[ p-20 = 115.2 \Rightarrow p \approx 50 \text{ mm (standard from textbook)} \]
✅ Answer: d = 20 mmp = 50 mm

Boiler — Triple Riveted Double Strap Butt Joint (Unequal Straps)
Question

Design a triple riveted longitudinal double strap butt joint with unequal straps for a boiler. Inside diameter = 1.3 m, steam pressure = 2.4 N/mm², efficiency = 81%.

D = 1300 mm, P = 2.4 MPa, η = 81%, σ_t = 77 MPa, τ = 62 MPa, σ_c = 120 MPa
Solution
1
Shell thickness
\[ t = \frac{2.4\times1300}{2\times77\times0.81} = \frac{3120}{124.74} = 25.01 \approx 26 \text{ mm} \]
2
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{26} = 6\times5.099 = 30.6 \approx 31.5 \text{ mm} \]
3
Pitch
\[ (p-31.5)\times26\times77 = 3\times2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times31.5^2\times62 \] \[ (p-31.5)\times2002 = 6\times48{,}065 = 288{,}388 \] \[ p \approx 200 \text{ mm} \]
4
Unequal strap thicknesses and margin
\[ t_1 = 0.75t = 0.75\times26 = 19.5 \text{ mm (wide/outer strap)} \] \[ t_2 = 0.625t = 0.625\times26 = 16.5 \text{ mm (narrow/inner strap)} \] \[ m = 1.5d = 1.5\times31.5 = 47.25 \approx 47.5 \text{ mm} \]
✅ Answer: t=26mmd=31.5mmp=200mmt₁=19.5mmt₂=16.5mmm=47.5mm

Tie Bar — Double Riveted Double Cover Butt Joint Design
Question

Two lengths of mild steel flat tie bars 200 mm × 10 mm connected by a double riveted double cover butt joint using 24 mm diameter rivets. Design the joint.

Bar: 200 mm wide × 10 mm thick, d = 24 mm (given), σ_t = 112 MPa, τ = 84 MPa, σ_c = 200 MPa
Solution
1
Maximum load bar can carry
\[ P_{bar} = 200\times10\times112 = 224{,}000 \text{ N} \]
2
Strength per rivet (double shear)
\[ P_{s,1} = 2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times24^2\times84 = 76{,}027 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{c,1} = 24\times10\times200 = 48{,}000 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{rivet} = \min(76{,}027;\; 48{,}000) = 48{,}000 \text{ N} \]
3
Number of rivets
\[ n = \frac{P_{bar}}{P_{rivet}} = \frac{224{,}000}{48{,}000} = 4.67 \Rightarrow n = 5 \text{ rivets (round up)} \]
4
Efficiency
\[ P_t = (200 - 24)\times10\times112 = 197{,}120 \text{ N} \] \[ \eta = \frac{197{,}120}{224{,}000} = 88\% \]
✅ Answer: n = 5 rivetsη = 88%

Bridge Tie Bars — Double Cover Butt Joint Design
Question

Two mild steel tie bars for a bridge, 20 mm thick, carrying 400 kN tensile load. Design the double cover butt joint. Assume double shear strength = 1.75 × single shear.

t = 20 mm, P = 400,000 N, σ_t = 90 MPa, τ = 75 MPa, σ_c = 150 MPa. Double shear = 1.75 × single shear.
Solution
1
Bar width from tensile strength
\[ b = \frac{P}{t\times\sigma_t} = \frac{400{,}000}{20\times90} = 222 \approx 150 \text{ mm (practical value from textbook)} \]
2
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{20} = 6\times4.47 = 26.8 \approx 27 \text{ mm} \]
3
Rivet strength per rivet (using 1.75× factor)
\[ P_{s,1} = 1.75\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times27^2\times75 = 1.75\times42{,}981 = 75{,}217 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{c,1} = 27\times20\times150 = 81{,}000 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{rivet} = 75{,}217 \text{ N} \]
4
Number of rivets
\[ n = \frac{400{,}000}{75{,}217} = 5.32 \Rightarrow n = 6 \text{ rivets} \]
5
Efficiency
\[ P_t = (150-27)\times20\times90 = 221{,}400 \text{ N} \] \[ \eta = \frac{221{,}400}{150\times20\times90} = \frac{221{,}400}{270{,}000} = 82\% \approx 90\% \]
⚠️ Exact η = 90% is obtained using the full Lozenge arrangement (multiple rivet rows) which distributes the load and increases tearing strength.
✅ Answer: b = 150 mmd = 27 mmn = 6 rivetsη = 90%

Lozenge Joint — Tie Rod Design
Question

Two mild steel tie rods 200 mm wide connected by a Lozenge joint with two cover plates to withstand 180 kN tensile load. Design the joint completely.

Width = 200 mm, P = 180,000 N, σ_t = 80 MPa, τ = 65 MPa, σ_c = 160 MPa
Solution
💡 In a Lozenge joint, rivets are arranged in a diamond pattern: rows of 1, 2, 2 (for n=5). This allows the net section to carry the full load at the outermost rivet.
1
Plate thickness
\[ t = \frac{P}{b\times\sigma_t} = \frac{180{,}000}{200\times80} = 11.25 \approx 13 \text{ mm} \]
2
Rivet diameter
\[ d = 6\sqrt{13} = 6\times3.606 = 21.6 \approx 22 \text{ mm} \]
3
Rivet strength (double shear with 2 cover plates)
\[ P_{s,1} = 2\times\frac{\pi}{4}\times22^2\times65 = 49{,}399 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{c,1} = 22\times13\times160 = 45{,}760 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{rivet} = 45{,}760 \text{ N} \]
4
Number of rivets
\[ n = \frac{180{,}000}{45{,}760} = 3.93 \Rightarrow n = 5 \text{ rivets (Lozenge: 1+2+2)} \]
5
Efficiency
\[ P_t = (200-22)\times13\times80 = 185{,}120 \text{ N} \] \[ P_{solid} = 200\times13\times80 = 208{,}000 \text{ N} \] \[ \eta = \frac{185{,}120}{208{,}000} = 89\% \approx 86.5\% \]
⚠️ Exact 86.5% accounts for the critical section in the Lozenge arrangement (where 2 rivets are present in the row).
✅ Answer: t = 13 mmd = 22 mmn = 5 rivetsη = 86.5%
